## The problem

I wanted to understand how a database actually puts bytes on disk and finds them
again — not at the SQL layer, but underneath it, at the page and index level.

## The approach

I started at the storage layer: a fixed-size page is the unit of I/O, and a slotted
directory inside each page tracks where every record lives. On top of that sits a B+
tree for ordered lookups, and a write-ahead log for durability.

## Where it stands

The storage engine and B+ tree inserts work; the WAL and crash recovery are in
progress. The build logs live in the **Building a Mini DBMS** blog series.
