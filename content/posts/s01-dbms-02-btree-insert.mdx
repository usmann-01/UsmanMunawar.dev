Inserting into a B+ tree is easy until a node fills up. Then you *split*, and the
split is where I lost an afternoon.

## The median pick

When a leaf overflows you push the median key up to the parent. My bug: I copied the
median into the right node **and** left it in the left node.

```python
mid = len(keys) // 2
left, right = keys[:mid], keys[mid:]  # median stays in `right`, promoted by copy
```

The fix was deciding, once, whether the median lives in the right node (leaf split)
or moves up entirely (internal split). Those are different rules and conflating them
is the classic mistake.

## Takeaways

- Leaf split: median is *copied* up, stays in the right leaf.
- Internal split: median *moves* up, lives in neither child.
